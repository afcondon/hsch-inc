### Gating questions — resolved (with one substantive note)

1. **Per-voice transform identity**: distinct, even when transforms equal. Voice is identity.
2. **Routing-default for un-bound voices**: silent. Warning hook is a future addition.
3. **`gate` missing-key default**: open — adding a voice can't be silently muted by a stale gate.
4. **`alternate` cycle granularity**: per-cycle (matches upstream `<a b c>`). Andrew flagged a deeper structural concern: Tidal's primitives are bar-scale, but he wants compositional structure at section/piece scale (10s of seconds to 10s of minutes). This isn't a question 4 problem per se — it's a separate primitive class to design. Surfaced concretely by Case A in BranchedHardCases (see below).
5. **Cell evaluator (Layer 2) wiring**: deferred until algebra settles.

### Surfaced future-primitive surfaces (from hard cases)

Three concrete things the hard-case cells surfaced as missing:

1. **`arrange` / `timeCat`** for weighted section-scale concatenation. The natural form is `arrange [(16, verse), (16, chorus), (8, bridge)]`. Available now: `cat` (equal weights, one cycle each) + `slow`, which forces a replicate-then-slow workaround. This is the substrate piece that turns Tidal from bar-scale to piece-scale — Andrew's question-4 concern in concrete form.
2. **Tree-shaped Branched** for inter-voice edges. Surfaced by Case B (call-and-response): flat fan-out can't represent "voice B derives from voice A" — both come out as parallel branches of one fan-out, making the dependency invisible to the renderer. This argues for moving nesting up the priority list. `Tree Voice (Pattern a)` with edges labelled by transforms is the natural shape.
3. **"During cycles a..b"** Boolean-pattern primitive. Surfaced by Case F (time-varying voice mute): expressible via `whenMod` with a predicate, but the form is fragile and high-ceremony. A clearer primitive belongs in Pattern.Core, not Branched.

### Things that worked fluently

- **Same fan-out, different merges per section** — the verse/chorus/bridge use case from the design conversation reads beautifully. `merge ensemble` for verse, `gate gmap ensemble` for chorus, `crossfade schedule ensemble` for bridge — same `ensemble`, three different merges. This is the "compositional thinking" payoff and it's confirmed in code.
- **`mult` subsumes `jux` cleanly** — three-way fan-out (Fugue Machine) is a one-liner.
- **Reusing one Branched under two unrelated downstream uses** — the inspectable middle representation pays off; Case E shows two independent merges of the same fan-out.
- **Nested fan-out via inner `merge`** — works *because* the inner merge collapses to a Pattern. The cost: inner topology is invisible to the renderer (the future Tree variant would preserve it).

### Things that didn't (and shouldn't, per design)

- Inter-voice references (Case B): out of scope for flat Branched, motivates Tree.
- Voice-conditional behaviour (Case C): would require a graph with feedback edges, which is more than fork/merge. Out of scope.

## Decisions Made

- **`fork-merge-design` branch is the working branch.** Three commits in. Don't merge yet — the type may still move based on Layer-2 wiring or further hard cases.
- **Section-scale arrangement is a separate primitive class.** Not a Branched concern. Will surface as `arrange`/`timeCat` in `Tidal.Pattern.Core` (and possibly a new `Tidal.Pattern.Section` module). Highest-value extension surfaced today.
- **Tree-shaped Branched is on the near horizon.** Case B's inability to represent inter-voice derivation is a real cost; nesting may move from "deferred" to "next" once the flat surface settles.
- **`voiced` is the canonical pair-builder.** Don't sprinkle `Tuple (Voice "...")` through cells.

## Evening session — host-language layer (option 3) for cells

After the v1 sketch landed, the gap surfaced clearly: cells today only
take mini-notation, so `Branched` was reachable only from PureScript
code. Without the host-language layer, the whole vocabulary was
shelfware. Plan went in
[`docs/kb/plans/purerl-tidal-host-language.md`](../kb/plans/purerl-tidal-host-language.md).

### What landed

- **`src/Tidal/Expr.purs`** — AST (`EVar` / `ENum` / `EStr` / `EApp` /
  `EList` / `ETag`), recursive-descent parser, evaluator with
  registry. Step 1 covered `id` / `rev` / `palindrome` / `slow` /
  `fast` / `every`. Step 2 added `jux` / `mult` / `alternate` /
  `crossfade` / `gate`. Bare names `true` / `false` desugar to
  `pure true` / `pure false` in `Pattern Boolean` for gate maps.
- **`Test/ExprSpec.purs`** — 35 tests: parse smokes (string, number,
  rational, negative, applications, list, tagged list, junk reject,
  unterminated string), eval roundtrips that compare event counts
  against direct PureScript calls, error paths for unknown function /
  wrong arity / non-int to `every` / bad mini-notation / non-function
  to `jux` / non-list spec / untagged list entry / non-bool gate
  value / missing voice pattern. Suite: 298 / 0.
- **`Scheduler.Msg`** — new `UpdateGateTrackP Int (Pattern String)`
  variant carrying a pre-built `Pattern` closure. Added the no-op
  arm to the base scheduler and the real arm to `MIDIScheduler` —
  same as `UpdateGateTrack` but skips the mini-notation parse step.
- **`Handler.erl`** — `gate <ch> :<expr>` recognised in
  `handle_pattern_message`. Strips the leading `:`, calls
  `('tidal_expr@ps':eval())(ExprSrc)`, dispatches `Right Pat` to the
  scheduler as `{updateGateTrackP, Ch, Pat}` and `Left Err` back to
  the WS client as `ERROR: expr: ...`. Crash protection in case the
  closure surface throws.

### End-to-end smoke (no synth, just wire shape)

Started the server, sent six expression forms via raw WebSocket

Calypso → Laplace test deferred to next session with the rig up.
